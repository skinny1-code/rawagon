Onboarding Process

From signup to first transaction in under an hour

No hardware to buy. No developer needed. No downtime. You never shut down your existing payments — QWKS runs alongside them and you migrate at your own pace.

01
Register your business on-chain
Go to qwks.io/business. Enter your business name, EIN/tax ID, address, and industry. QWKS deploys a Business Registry NFT on Base L2 — your unique on-chain identity. This is your business's permanent, tamper-proof registration. No one can impersonate your business on the network.
Base L2 Free 3 minutes
02
Install the payment widget
Copy and paste a single JavaScript snippet into your website, POS system, or checkout flow. Alternatively, use the QWKS mobile app as a standalone terminal. The widget accepts: AllCard tap, USDC, LTN, or a QR code scan that generates a one-time payment proof. Your existing Stripe/Square terminal continues working in parallel — switch customers over on your own timeline.
1 line of code Works with existing POS
03
Migrate employee records to smart contracts
Upload your existing HR data (CSV or manual entry). QWKS encrypts each employee's record and stores it in a EmployeeVault smart contract. The contract only releases data when the employee's AllCard/ZK proof authorizes it — no one else can access their records, including you, without their consent. Payroll, contracts, ID verification, and benefits are all handled through the same contract.
ZK proof required Smart contract CCPA compliant
04
Issue AllCards to employees
Each employee gets a 1.nce AllCard account. Their identity is ZK-verified (no raw data stored anywhere). Payroll can be sent directly to their AllCard — instant, $0.00084 per disbursement. Tax documents (W-2, 1099) are stored in their AllCard vault, accessible only to them and authorized entities with their consent.
1.nce AllCard $0.00084/payroll transfer
05
Customer records on smart contracts
Customers with AllCards authenticate themselves to your business using a ZK proof — you receive confirmation of their identity, age, or payment eligibility without ever seeing or storing their raw data. No PII database for you to protect. No GDPR liability. No breach risk for customer data because you never hold it.

Smart Contract Data Architecture

How your data is protected

Smart contracts are self-executing code on the blockchain. They hold rules — not data. Your employee's Social Security number never touches any server. Their smart contract only ever confirms "this person is authorized" — and only when they prove it themselves.

EmployeeVault.sol — Employee Data Contract
01contract EmployeeVault {
02// No raw data stored here. Ever.
03mapping(address => bytes32) commitment;
04// commitment = hash(encrypted_vault_root)
05
06function verifyEmployee(
07bytes32 zkProof,
08uint8 scope // 1=active, 2=payroll, 3=full
09) external view returns (bool) {
10// Verify ZK proof matches commitment
11return verifyZKProof(zkProof, commitment[msg.sender]);
12}
13// Raw SSN, DOB, address: NEVER on-chain
14}

Optional Integrations

Works standalone. Better together.

QWKS Business Protocol is a complete, independent product. These integrations are available when you want them — each one is separate, each one optional.

Architecture principle

Every product in the RAWagon ecosystem is a separate entity on the chain. QWKS Business Protocol, BitPawn, 1.nce AllCard, AutoIQ, and GoldSnap each have their own smart contracts, their own tokens, and their own operations. They can communicate — but they never depend on each other. Your QWKS payments work whether or not BitPawn is running. Your AllCard works whether or not QWKS is running. No single point of failure anywhere.
